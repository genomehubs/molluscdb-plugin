(From Wikipedia) The Mediterranean mussel (Mytilus galloprovincialis) is a species of marine bivalve mollusc in the family Mytilidae. It is an invasive species in many parts of the world, and also an object of aquaculture.

Picture credit: Wikimedia Commons

Assembly

Transcriptome assembly generated by the MolluscDB team using publicly available data [1]:

Study: SRP063654

Selected runs: SRR2392495, SRR2392762, SRR2409049

The quality of the dataset was first assessed with FastQC v0.11.5 [2]. Then, raw reads were quality and adapter trimmed with BBDuk v36.92 [3] (bbduk ... ref=adapters.fa threads=16 ktrim=r k=23 mink=11 hammingdistance=1 ftm=5 tpe tbo qtrim=rl trimq=5 minavgquality=10 minlength=25). Finally, trimmed reads were de novo assembled with Trinity v2.4.0 [4] (Trinity --seqType fq --max_memory 128G --CPU 16 --left ... --right ...).

Annotation

Transcripts were annotated by the MolluscDB team with TransDecoder v3.0.1 [5]. BLASTp v2.6.0+ [6] searches against UniRef90 release 2017_03 [7] (blastp -query longest_orfs.pep -db uniref90.fasta -max_target_seqs 1 -outfmt 6 -evalue 1e-5 -out blastp.outfmt6), and hmmscan v3.1b2 [8] searches against Pfam v31.0 [9] (hmmscan --cpu 16 --domtblout pfam.domtblout Pfam-A.hmm longest_orfs.pep) were included in the TransDecoder pipeline to maximize sensitivity for capturing ORFs (TransDecoder.LongOrfs -t Trinity.fasta; TransDecoder.Predict --cpu 16 --single_best_orf -t Trinity.fasta --retain_pfam_hits pfam.domtblout --retain_blastp_hits blastp.outfmt6).
