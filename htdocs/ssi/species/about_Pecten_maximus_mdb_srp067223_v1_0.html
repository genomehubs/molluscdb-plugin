(From Wikipedia) The king scallop (Pecten maximus) is an edible saltwater clam, a marine bivalve mollusc in the family Pectinidae. Pecten maximus occurs in the eastern Atlantic along the European coast from northern Norway, south to the Iberian peninsula, it has also been reported off West Africa, off the Macaronesian Islands. In Great Britain and Ireland it is distributed all round the coast but it is uncommon and localised on the eastern North Sea coast.

Assembly

Transcriptome assembly generated by the MolluscDB team using publicly available data [1]:

Study: SRP067223

Selected runs: SRR3101476, SRR3101475, SRR3101480, SRR3101477, SRR3101483, SRR3101479, SRR3101487, SRR3101482, SRR3101481, SRR3101474

The quality of the dataset was first assessed with FastQC v0.11.5 [2]. Then, raw reads were quality and adapter trimmed with BBDuk v36.92 [3] (bbduk ... ref=adapters.fa threads=16 ktrim=r k=23 mink=11 hammingdistance=1 ftm=5 tpe tbo qtrim=rl trimq=5 minavgquality=10 minlength=25). Finally, trimmed reads were de novo assembled with Trinity v2.4.0 [4] (Trinity --seqType fq --max_memory 128G --CPU 16 --left ... --right ... --SS_lib_type RF).

Annotation

Transcripts were annotated by the MolluscDB team with TransDecoder v3.0.1 [5]. BLASTp v2.6.0+ [6] searches against UniRef90 release 2017_03 [7] (blastp -query longest_orfs.pep -db uniref90.fasta -max_target_seqs 1 -outfmt 6 -evalue 1e-5 -out blastp.outfmt6), and hmmscan v3.1b2 [8] searches against Pfam v31.0 [9] (hmmscan --cpu 16 --domtblout pfam.domtblout Pfam-A.hmm longest_orfs.pep) were included in the TransDecoder pipeline to maximize sensitivity for capturing ORFs (TransDecoder.LongOrfs -t Trinity.fasta -S; TransDecoder.Predict --cpu 16 --single_best_orf -t Trinity.fasta --retain_pfam_hits pfam.domtblout --retain_blastp_hits blastp.outfmt6).
