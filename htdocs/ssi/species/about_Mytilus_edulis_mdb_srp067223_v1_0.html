(From MarLIN) The blue mussel (Mytilus edulis), is a sspecies of edible marine bivalve mollusc in the family Mytilidae. M. edulis occurs from the high intertidal to the shallow subtidal attached by fibrous byssus threads to suitable substrata. It can be found on the rocky shores of open coasts attached to the rock surface and in crevices, and on rocks and piers in sheltered harbours and estuaries, often occurring as dense masses. Its distribution ranges from the White Sea, south to southern France in the N.E. Atlantic. In the W. Atlantic it extends from the Canadian Maritimes south to North Carolina. It occurs on the coasts of Chile, Argentina, the Falkland Islands and the Kerguelen Isles.

Assembly

Transcriptome assembly produced by the molluscdb team using public data:

Study: SRP067223
Selected runs: SRR3101489, SRR3101492, SRR3101494, SRR3101495, SRR3101496, SRR3101497, SRR3101501

The quality of the dataset was first assessed with FastQC v0.11.5 [1]. Then, raw reads were quality and adapter trimmed with BBDuk v36.92 [2] (bbduk ... ref=adapters.fa threads=16 ktrim=r k=23 mink=11 hammingdistance=1 ftm=5 tpe tbo qtrim=rl trimq=5 minavgquality=10 minlength=25). Finally, trimmed reads were de novo assembled with Trinity v2.4.0 [3] (Trinity --seqType fq --max_memory 128G --CPU 16 --left ... --right ... --SS_lib_type RF).

Annotation

Transcripts were annotated by the molluscDB team with TransDecoder v3.0.1 [4]. BLASTp v2.6.0+ [5] searches against UniRef90 release 2017_03 (blastp -query longest_orfs.pep -db uniref90.fasta -max_target_seqs 1 -outfmt 6 -evalue 1e-5 -out blastp.outfmt6), and hmmscan v3.1b2 [6] searches against Pfam v31.0 (hmmscan --cpu 16 --domtblout pfam.domtblout Pfam-A.hmm longest_orfs.pep) were included in the TransDecoder pipeline to maximize sensitivity for capturing ORFs (TransDecoder.LongOrfs -t Trinity.fasta -S; TransDecoder.Predict --cpu 16 --single_best_orf -t Trinity.fasta --retain_pfam_hits pfam.domtblout --retain_blastp_hits blastp.outfmt6).
