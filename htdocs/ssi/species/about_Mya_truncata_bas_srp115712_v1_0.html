From Wikipedia: Mya truncata, common name the blunt gaper or truncate softshell, is a species of edible saltwater clam, a marine bivalve mollusc in the family Myidae.

Assembly

This resource is unpublished, please contact Victoria A. Sleight, British Antarctic Survey, before using it.

The quality of the reads was first assessed with FastQC v0.11.5 [1]. Adapter sequences were trimmed, and low quality and short reads were removed with Fastq-Mcf v1.1.2 [2] (fastq-mcf ... -q 30 -l 80 adapters.fa). Cleaned reads were normalised with Trinity's (v2.2.0) [3] in silico read normalisation tool using default parameters. Then, reads were assembled with Trinity v2.2.0 [3] (Trinity --seqType fq --max_memory 200G --CPU 16 --left ... --right ... --SS_lib_type RF). Finally, contigs shorter than 300 bp were removed from the assembly.

Annotation

Transcripts were annotated by the MolluscDB team with TransDecoder v3.0.1 [4]. BLASTp v2.6.0+ [5] searches against UniRef90 release 2017_03 [6] (blastp -query longest_orfs.pep -db uniref90.fasta -max_target_seqs 1 -outfmt 6 -evalue 1e-5 -out blastp.outfmt6), and hmmscan v3.1b2 [7] searches against Pfam v31.0 [8] (hmmscan --cpu 16 --domtblout pfam.domtblout Pfam-A.hmm longest_orfs.pep) were included in the TransDecoder pipeline to maximize sensitivity for capturing ORFs (TransDecoder.LongOrfs -t Trinity.fasta; TransDecoder.Predict --cpu 16 --single_best_orf -t Trinity.fasta --retain_pfam_hits pfam.domtblout --retain_blastp_hits blastp.outfmt6).
