From Wikipedia: The vampire squid (Vampyroteuthis infernalis) is a small, deep-sea cephalopod found throughout the temperate and tropical oceans of the world. Unique retractile sensory filaments justify the vampire squid's placement in its own order: Vampyromorphida (formerly Vampyromorpha), which shares similarities with both squid and octopodes. As a phylogenetic relict it is the only known surviving member of its order.

Annotation

Transcripts were annotated by the molluscDB team with TransDecoder v3.0.1 [2]. BLASTp v2.6.0+ [3] searches against UniRef90 release 2017_03 [4] (blastp -query longest_orfs.pep -db uniref90.fasta -max_target_seqs 1 -outfmt 6 -evalue 1e-5 -out blastp.outfmt6), and hmmscan v3.1b2 [5] searches against Pfam v31.0 [6] (hmmscan --cpu 16 --domtblout pfam.domtblout Pfam-A.hmm longest_orfs.pep) were included in the TransDecoder pipeline to maximize sensitivity for capturing ORFs (TransDecoder.LongOrfs -t Trinity.fasta; TransDecoder.Predict --cpu 16 --single_best_orf -t Trinity.fasta --retain_pfam_hits pfam.domtblout --retain_blastp_hits blastp.outfmt6).
